Margaret Holloway (born 12 March 1954) is a British marine biologist known for her research on cold-water coral ecosystems in the North Atlantic. She served as director of the Scottish Association for Marine Science from 2001 to 2009, where she led several long-term monitoring programmes on deep-sea habitats. Holloway studied zoology at the University of Aberdeen, completing her doctorate in 1981 with a thesis on the feeding behaviour of Lophelia pertusa. Her early work focused on the distribution of coral reefs along the continental shelf, and she later contributed to the designation of several marine protected areas off the west coast of Scotland. She was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh in 2004 and received the Challenger Medal in 2011. After retiring from active fieldwork, Holloway remained involved in marine policy, advising government bodies on the environmental impact of bottom trawling. She has published more than 120 papers and supervised over 30 doctoral students during her career.
